A guide for Mrs Froggy — everything you need to know, ribbit.

What even is this?

This is your personal fitness dashboard — it takes all the data your watch quietly collects (runs, rides, sleep, heart rate, stress, recovery) and turns it into proper charts you can actually learn from.

Garmin Connect shows you some of this, but it hides old data, caps chart ranges, and generally makes it annoying to see the big picture. This app has no caps and no limits. You can look at every run from the last five years side by side if you like.

🐸 Froggy note: There's nothing to install or set up — that part's already done. Just double-click the My Fitness icon and your dashboard opens in your normal web browser.

Opening the app

Double-click the My Fitness icon on your desktop or Start Menu. A browser tab opens automatically with your dashboard — that's it, no login screen, no loading wait.

The app itself keeps running quietly in the background after that, with a small frog icon in your system tray (bottom-right of the screen, near the clock — click the ^ arrow if you don't see it). You can close the browser tab any time without closing the app; just double-click the tray icon again whenever you want the dashboard back.

⚠️ To fully quit: closing the browser tab does not shut the app down — it just keeps syncing quietly in the tray. Right-click the tray icon and choose Quit if you ever need to close it completely.

Keeping your data fresh

Your watch data syncs automatically every night — no button to press. If you want the latest numbers right now (say, just after a run), right-click the tray icon and choose Sync Now. You'll get a little Windows notification when it's finished (or if something went wrong).

Getting around

A menu bar across the top lists every page in the app. On a smaller window it tucks itself behind a ☰ button — tap that to see the same list. Wherever you are, an "Ask AI" button is always nearby if you'd rather just ask a question than hunt for a chart.

What's on each page

🏠

Dashboard

Your daily overview: resting heart rate, HRV, sleep, stress, body battery, steps and intensity minutes. Hide or show each chart, pick a date range, and it remembers your last view next time.

🏃

Activities

A searchable, sortable list of every workout. Filter by type, date or name, then click one to open its full detail.

🗺️

Activity Detail

Everything about one workout: pace, heart rate, elevation, splits, running form, and a colour-coded map of the route. Hover the chart and the map highlights the same spot, and back again.

📦

Volume

How much distance, time and elevation you've covered over any date range, broken down by activity type.

📈

Performance

Race-readiness and pace trends over time.

❤️

Intensity

Time spent in each heart rate zone — as hours, percentages, or a trend. Handy for checking your training balance.

🦵

Dynamics

Running form: ground contact time, cadence, stride length, left/right balance and power.

⚡

Efficiency

How your pace at a given heart rate is trending — a rising line means getting fitter for the same effort.

🏋️

Load

Weekly training load, monotony and strain — flags weeks that are too samey or too hard.

🔬

Analysis

Overlay up to five metrics on one chart, compare two date ranges side by side, or scatter-plot two metrics against each other.

🏆

Records

Your personal bests — fastest times, longest distances — each linking back to the activity that set it.

📌

Events

Add notes for races, injuries, illness or anything else worth remembering. These show up as markers on the other charts for context.

⏱️

Intraday

Pick a single day and see minute-by-minute heart rate, stress, steps and breathing rate.

🧭

Planner

Plan a route on the map, see live distance, estimated time, ascent and an elevation profile, then save it or export it to your watch.

💬

Chat

Ask the AI assistant questions about your data in plain English.

📋

Training

Describe a goal and the AI builds you a week-by-week training plan, using your own recent fitness data to shape it. Tick off workouts as you complete them, edit or delete any that don't fit, and browse past plans.

⚙️

Settings

Picks which AI model powers Chat and Training. Just leave this one alone unless Froggy tells you otherwise.

Looking at one workout

Click any row on the Activities page to open its full detail: pace, heart rate, elevation, calories, splits, heart-rate zones, and (for runs) a running-form chart.

Below that sits a route map. Hover anywhere on the chart and the map jumps to that exact point on your route — and hovering the map does the same back to the chart. You can also choose what colours the route (pace, heart rate, elevation) and switch the map style.

Life events — what they are and why they matter

The Events page lets you add your own notes to the timeline — a label, a date or date range, and a note. These appear as vertical lines or shaded bands across the Dashboard, Performance and Analysis charts, so you can see at a glance why a chart dipped or spiked. Worth logging:

  • Races
  • Injuries — so you can see the dip in training load and recovery
  • Illness
  • Medication changes
  • Travel or anything else that might explain a blip in the data

Planning a route

The Planner page is an interactive map for plotting a route before you go:

  • Search for a location to centre the map
  • Click to drop waypoints — they snap to paths and roads automatically
  • Drag a point to adjust it, undo/redo, reverse the route, or clear it and start again
  • Live distance, an estimated time (you can set your target pace), ascent/descent and an elevation profile update as you go
  • Save a route with a name, reload it later, or delete it
  • Export the finished route as a file you can load onto your watch

Getting an AI training plan

The Training page builds you a personalised training plan:

  • Describe your goal (e.g. "10k in under 50 minutes"), pick a start and end date and how many days a week you can train
  • Click Autofill from my data and it pulls in your recent volume, longest run, VO2 max and personal bests automatically — tweak any of these by hand if they don't look right
  • Click Generate and the AI writes a week-by-week plan with a short explanation of its reasoning
  • Tick off each workout as you complete it, edit or delete any that don't suit you, then save the plan to make it active
  • Only one plan is active at a time — end it whenever you like, and past plans stay browsable further down the page

The AI Chat assistant

The "Ask AI" button opens a chat panel from anywhere in the app. Ask it things like "how has my resting heart rate trended this month?" or "what was my longest run last year?" — it knows which page you're on and what date range you've selected, and answers from your real data.

There's also a full Chat page for longer conversations, with a sidebar of past chats you can revisit or delete.

🐸 What it can and can't do: The AI can look up and explain your fitness data, but it can't change anything, log into Garmin, or see anything outside your own data. It always checks the real numbers before answering, so it won't make things up.
